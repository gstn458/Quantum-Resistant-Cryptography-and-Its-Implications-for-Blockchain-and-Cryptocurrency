About & Mission

Understand the math behind cryptography

CryptoMath exists to make the beautiful, abstract mathematics that secures the digital world feel intuitive — not something to memorise, but something to see.

Our mission

Most people use cryptography every day — every HTTPS padlock, every signed transaction — without ever seeing the elegant number theory underneath. We believe that understanding why these systems work makes them less intimidating and far more memorable than rote formulas.

So we teach the mathematics first: modular arithmetic, prime numbers, discrete logarithms, elliptic curves and the post-quantum horizon — each introduced through interactive tools, worked examples, and progressive practice rather than walls of notation.

How we teach — our pedagogy

🧭 Intuition before notation

Every concept starts with a concrete picture or interactive you can manipulate, then we formalise it.

🧩 Small, active steps

Lessons are broken into bite-sized steps with a quick check-for-understanding after each — learning by doing.

🔗 Connected, not isolated

A sequenced learning path with prerequisites, plus a glossary and cross-links so nothing is a dead end.

Rigor & sources

We aim to be mathematically faithful. The material here is grounded in standard references; if you want to go deeper, these are excellent next reads:

  • Katz & Lindell, Introduction to Modern Cryptography — the standard rigorous text.
  • Hoffstein, Pipher & Silverman, An Introduction to Mathematical Cryptography — number theory to lattices.
  • Menezes, van Oorschot & Vanstone, Handbook of Applied Cryptography — free online.
  • NIST Post-Quantum Cryptography project — standards & rationale.
  • Stanford CS255 & MIT OpenCourseWare cryptography materials — free lecture notes.
